Applied Materials (AMAT) is the world's largest semiconductor equipment company and one of the most deeply moated businesses in the registry. Its tools deposit, etch, implant, and planarize the atomic-scale material layers in every advanced chip — from AI GPUs at TSMC to DRAM at SK Hynix. Process recipe lock-in, co-development relationships embedded 3–5 years before node ramps, and approximately 70% global ion-implant share create switching barriers among the highest in any industry. The AGS services segment (~20% of revenue) provides a recurring revenue floor largely insulated from new-fab capex cycles. Decentralizability is near zero: leading-edge tools cost $3–60M, require aerospace-grade manufacturing precision, and embody decades of institutional materials science that no open-source or distributed community effort can replicate at production scale. The primary structural risks are Chinese domestic equipment firms (NAURA, AMEC) at mature nodes and US export controls that cap leading-edge China revenue. AI infrastructure buildout, gate-all-around transistors, advanced packaging, and SiC power semiconductors are the primary near-term growth catalysts.
