Applied Materials is the world's largest semiconductor equipment company, with a near-impenetrable moat built on multi-year equipment qualification barriers, a 60-year IP portfolio, and process recipe lock-in at every leading chipmaker globally. Its decentralizability score is among the lowest in the registry: there is no open or community path to replicating leading-edge chip fab equipment. The most meaningful alternative vectors operate at the design layer—open EDA tools (OpenROAD), open PDKs (SkyWater SKY130), and open FPGA toolchains (IceStorm/nextpnr) collectively lower the barrier for open-source silicon and reduce the fraction of electronics that must pass through Applied's tools. For display applications, e-paper provides a lower-energy, more accessible alternative for static display use cases where OLED's performance advantages are unnecessary.
