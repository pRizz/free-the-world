Applied Materials is the world's largest semiconductor equipment company by revenue (~$27B, FY2025), supplying the deposition, etch, CMP, and metrology tools that underpin virtually every advanced chip made globally. Its moat is among the deepest in the S&P 500: 12–18 month qualification cycles lock tools into fabs for 7–15 years, the Applied Global Services segment converts the installed base into a durable annuity, and decades of co-engineering with TSMC, Samsung, and Intel have created tacit process knowledge that cannot be purchased or replicated quickly. The company scores at the lowest decentralizability level in this registry—no open-source or distributed manufacturing alternative exists or is credibly under development for production-grade semiconductor equipment. The nearest open-source analogs (OpenROAD EDA, Efabless, SkyWater PDK) address the chip design layer only and depend entirely on physical fab equipment to produce silicon. Near-term risks center on U.S. export controls restricting access to the Chinese market and broader capital equipment cycle dynamics.
