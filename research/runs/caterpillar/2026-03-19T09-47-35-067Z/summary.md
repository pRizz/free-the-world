Caterpillar (CAT) is the world's dominant heavy equipment manufacturer with a century-old brand, an unmatched exclusive dealer network (~160 dealers, ~2,800 branches), and a compounding aftermarket business targeting $28B in services revenue by 2026. Its moat (8.5/10) is one of the most durable in industrials—grounded in physical service infrastructure and a mining autonomy data flywheel that cannot be quickly replicated. Its decentralizability (2/10) is among the lowest in the S&P 500: the physics of large mining and construction equipment preclude distributed or open-source alternatives at commercial scale. The AI data center buildout is a secular tailwind for Cat's power systems business, partially offsetting the 2024 construction market moderation. Open Source Ecology's GVCS represents the philosophical seed of decentralized machinery but is not a commercial-scale threat.
