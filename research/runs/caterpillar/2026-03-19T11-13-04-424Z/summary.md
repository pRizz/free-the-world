Caterpillar is the global benchmark in heavy construction and mining equipment, with a century-old brand and a ~160-dealer global network that creates compounding switching costs. Its aftermarket parts ecosystem, MineStar autonomy data flywheel (1B+ tonnes hauled), and Cat Financial arm generate recurring, high-margin revenue that buffers cyclical equipment sales. Decentralizability is near zero—ultra-class mining trucks and large excavators require industrial-scale manufacturing far beyond distributed production. The only credible decentralization vectors are additive manufacturing of spare parts (long-tail aftermarket), distributed renewable energy stacks that could displace Cat gensets over time, and cooperative ownership structures that shift economic control without changing the equipment supply chain.
