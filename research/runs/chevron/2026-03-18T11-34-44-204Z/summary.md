Chevron is a fully integrated energy major with deep moats in upstream resource rights, LNG export infrastructure, and global refining logistics. Its decentralizability score is low (18/100): core petroleum extraction, refining, and LNG operations cannot be community-replicated. The clearest displacement path runs through demand substitution—rooftop solar paired with home battery stacks, OCPP-standard EV charging networks, and open-source energy monitoring progressively reduce individual dependence on Chevron's retail fuel and lubricant products. Community biogas and RNG offer partial long-term substitutes for natural gas supply. The distributed-energy technology wave is the primary structural force contesting Chevron's consumer-facing product lines over the coming decade.
