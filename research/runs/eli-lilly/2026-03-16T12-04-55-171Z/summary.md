Eli Lilly is a high-moat pharmaceutical company whose economic durability rests almost entirely on patent-protected branded drugs, most importantly the tirzepatide franchise (Mounjaro for type 2 diabetes, Zepbound for obesity). With gross margins above 75%, a multi-decade R&D engine, and what may be the largest addressable market in pharmaceutical history (GLP-1-driven obesity treatment), Lilly commands one of the highest valuations in the S&P 500. Decentralizability is effectively the lowest possible for a large-cap company: pharmaceutical manufacturing requires centralized GMP facilities, regulatory approvals, and cold-chain logistics that cannot be meaningfully disrupted by any current or foreseeable open-source technology wave. The primary decentralization mechanism is time-gated generic competition after patent expiry—relevant only in the 2030s for Lilly's core tirzepatide assets.
