Bank of America is one of the largest US diversified banks, serving ~69 million consumer and small-business clients through consumer banking, Merrill wealth management, and global banking and markets. Its moat is deep — built on sticky banking relationships, regulatory barriers, branch and ATM scale, and the Merrill Lynch franchise — scoring 8/10. Decentralizability scores 3/10 today: Bitcoin and Lightning can theoretically replace savings and payments, robo-advisors erode advisory margins, but credit, mortgages, and institutional banking remain very hard to decentralize. Market cap of ~$320B places it in the S&P 500 ranks 28–32 range as of March 2026.
