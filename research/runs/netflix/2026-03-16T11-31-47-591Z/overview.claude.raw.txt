        "Netflix operates on a direct-to-consumer subscription model, charging monthly fees tiered by plan features—ad-supported Standard, ad-free Standard, and Premium. Revenue is overwhelmingly recurring: subscribers pay regardless of how much content they consume, which smooths cash flows but creates constant pressure to justify renewal against an expanding field of competitors.",
        "The advertising tier, launched in late 2022 and accelerating through 2024-2025, represents a structural pivot. Netflix spent its first decade arguing that ads were beneath it; it now courts brand advertisers with targeting data derived from viewing behavior across roughly 300 million global accounts. The irony is noted, mostly by Netflix itself in earnings calls."

Its advantages are recommendation quality, content volume, and the compounding data flywheel from two decades of viewing history. Competitors—Disney+, Max, Amazon Prime Video, Apple TV+—are all attached to larger conglomerates that can subsidize streaming losses indefinitely, which caps Netflix's pricing power.",

      "title": "Bitcoin and Lightning Wave Exposure",
      "paragraphs": [
        "Netflix's subscription and advertising model is a centralized tollbooth on content access—exactly the architecture that protocol-native micropayment layers are theoretically designed to route around. A viewer paying per episode or per minute over Lightning would have no need for a subscription; a creator distributing directly to a Lightning-enabled client would have no need for Netflix's licensing terms.",
        "In practice, this threat is distant. Content rights, production financing, and marketing relationships are deeply intermediated, and no Lightning-native content platform has demonstrated the catalog depth or UI polish to compete. The more proximate risk is that Bitcoin-era anti-spam economics eventually pressure the advertising tier: if attention becomes measurably costly to fake, the programmatic ad ecosystem Netflix is joining becomes less lucrative for late entrants. Netflix is not positioned to benefit from this wave and has no disclosed interest in it."
