## Company Thesis

- Business mix
  Accenture is a global professional services company organized around consulting, managed services, technology, operations, and customer-experience work. Its fiscal 2025 annual report showed $69.7 billion of revenue and $10.2 billion of operating income, indicating a large, profitable services franchise rather than a single-product software platform.
  The company's moat comes from enterprise account access, implementation scale, delivery talent, relationships with major cloud and enterprise software vendors, and the switching costs embedded in large transformation programs.
- Registry relevance
  Accenture is a useful registry target because its products are not consumer software but bundled transformation practices that influence which infrastructure, cloud, analytics, marketing, and AI stacks large institutions adopt.
  Free and open alternatives can pressure pieces of the Accenture model when clients standardize on open infrastructure, self-hosted analytics, federated delivery teams, and transparent implementation playbooks instead of relying entirely on proprietary integrator-led programs.

### Moat narrative
- Accenture's moat is high because it sells trust, coordination, scale, and risk absorption to enterprises that cannot easily replace a large integrator with one tool. The company can bundle strategy, implementation, managed services, talent, offshore delivery, procurement relationships, and executive relationships into long-running accounts.
- The moat is not absolute. Open-source infrastructure, reusable implementation patterns, AI-assisted delivery, and smaller specialist networks can unbundle specific workstreams, especially when clients have enough internal capability to own architecture and vendor selection.

### Decentralization narrative
- Accenture is moderately decentralizable because many deliverables are knowledge work, integration work, and operational playbooks that can be produced by smaller firms or open communities. The strongest decentralizing pressure comes from open cloud-native infrastructure, infrastructure-as-code, self-hosted analytics, and transparent delivery standards.
- The hard-to-decentralize portion is enterprise accountability. Large clients often pay Accenture to coordinate many vendors, manage political risk, staff globally, and absorb delivery blame. Decentralized alternatives must therefore solve governance, verification, procurement, and support, not just provide open software.

## Replacement Sketch

- A lightweight replacement path combines open publishing, self-hosted analytics, portable customer data practices, and independent creative or implementation teams that leave clients with owned assets instead of agency-controlled operating knowledge.
- This does not replace global brand transformation in one step. It first replaces the repeatable substrate: websites, content operations, analytics, experimentation, and customer journey instrumentation.
