- The moat is reinforced by long-lived brands, a global installed base, recurring parts and service revenue, critical utility relationships, and smart-metering data infrastructure. ### Decentralization narrative
- Xylem is only moderately decentralizable at the company level because much of its value is embedded in certified hardware, field service, utility procurement, meter accuracy, water-sector liability, and mission-critical infrastructure.
- The most plausible pressure comes from layers around the equipment: open PLCs for non-critical controls, EPANET-style hydraulic modeling, customer-owned energy and water telemetry, open demand-response standards, and cooperative service records. Those layers can reduce lock-in and expose performance gaps before they displace core pumps or revenue-grade smart meters.

## Replacement Sketch

- A realistic open replacement path starts around the pump station, not with a direct clone of every submersible wastewater pump.
